Eat Healthy With Us

Why eat healthy
Good for mind & body - reduces risk of cancer, health diseases
About 34% of all human-caused greenhouse gas emissions is linked to food.

Extreme weather

Global warming results in higher frequency and intensity of droughts, storms heat waves.

Habitat destruction

Increased temperature leads to wildfires, melting of glaciers which destroys the environment that creatures reside in.

Rising sea levels

As glaciers melt and sea levels rise, beaches and low-rising land are threatened by risk of flooding.

"The scientific evidence is unequivocal: climate change is a threat to human wellbeing and the health of the planet. Any further delay in concerted global action will miss the brief, rapidly closing window to secure a liveable future."

- Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change

What we can do to help

Adopt sustainable habits. By being more environmentally conscious in our daily life, we can help to reduce the growth of global warming. Every effort counts.

How our eating habits affect the environment

Food needs to be grown and processed, transported, distributed, prepared, consumed, and sometimes disposed of.

Each of these steps creates greenhouse gases that trap the sun's heat and contribute to climate change.
What you eat matters

Animal-based foods

Generally associated with the high greenhouse gas emissions.

Plant-based foods

Generally use less energy, land, and water, and have lower greenhouse gas intensities.
By switching out about
10 percent of daily calorie intake
It cuts a person's food-based environmental footprint
By over 30 percent

Eat better for the environment

Improve your health and protect the environment at the same time by shifting to a plant-based diet.
